McMillen Web

Hello, my name is Lauren McMillen, and I am a junior developer from Denver, Colorado. After beginning my first website in June 2018, I have fallen in love with learning new methods of web design. I hope to improve my web development skills through any project I can!

I am currently a sophomore at New York University Abu Dhabi, where I major in Computer Science and minor in Arabic. I have a passion for language and culture, especially in the Arab World, and I absolutely love to travel!

I want to make a career in using computer science to solve humanitarian problems. My recent exploration- and subsequent love- for web development has steered me towards web development for non-profits. I'm excited to learn about how a non-profit's online presence brings in funds and awareness. My long-term goal is to focus on creating unique web and mobile applications for social good. The first step towards that goal is the web-based non-profit I am founding, SmallWorld.

Non-Profit Experience

Starting in 8th grade, I have been involved with the Nyaka AIDS Orphans Project. What began at age 13 as bake sales outside a grocery store became a trip to Uganda at age 17 to install solar panels purchased after a year-long fundraiser- and the realization that I want to work in the non-profit and human rights field for the rest of my life.

This past summer, I volunteered for 3 weeks at Care4Calais, a charity in Calais, France, which distributes food and clothing to the migrants there. While I learned a huge amount about the refugee crisis, the major lesson I felt I could act on was that small charities could benefit hugely from greater online integration. I brought the idea, for example, of creating an e-store for the used goods, not useful to the migrants, which sat in the Care4Calais warehouse.

After our experience in high school, my friend and I were frustrated with the fact that high schoolers can make a real impact, but either they don't know that or others don't believe it. SmallWorld is our solution. Though in its early stages, SmallWorld's website will become a platform for student teams to learn about humanitarian problems, identify problems to solve, and collaborate with each other.
